2. Wild Cherry

Heat 25
NYC · Eater

The duo behind Tribeca’s Frenchette and Upper West Side revival Le Veau d’Or , chefs Riad Nasr and Lee Hanson, teamed up with indie film studio A24 ( Moonlight , Past Lives ) to create the buzziest downtown restaurant in October 2025. Attached to the reopened West Village Cherry Lane Theatre , every seat at Wild Cherry is a good one, even the tiny horseshoe bar, so you’ll have a high chance of celebrity-spotting from the likes of Bradley Cooper and Sabrina Carpenter while enjoying a varied menu ranging from frogs legs Kiev to standbys like an excellent cheeseburger.

60. Hwaro

Heat 20
NYC · Eater

Chef Sungchul Shim’s most intimate project yet is tucked inside his Times Square steakhouse, Gui , which opened in October 2025. At the 22-seat marble counter of Hwaro , Shim leans into Korean technique and heritage through a tasting menu built around a custom charcoal grill — the restaurant’s namesake braiser. The 13-course $295 menu is served around the circular counter, with dishes like foie gras served with duck terrine, mushroom pies, and scallops with truffles and makgeolli bearnaise espuma. There’s also a nine-course option for $245.

Reservations: Moderate — Book ahead where possible; otherwise aim for off-peak (early/late) and consider bar seating.

61. Lalyn

Heat 20
NYC · Eater

The restaurateur behind NYC Thai restaurants Malii Gramercy and When in Bangkok , Olie Sangpetpairot, expanded with this new restaurant, which opened in December 2025. The menu, from executive chef Kitipoom “Nicky” Khanarat and head chef Supattra “Tina” Thipdong, leans into a pan-regional approach done with contemporary touches, with items like scallop crudo with tom kha sorbet, pad Thai with shrimp tamarind sauce, and fish curry gratin, along with cocktails with pandan, chiles, and palm sugar. The decor for the spacious dining room takes its cues from its name, which is a Thai word for “moon,” leading to a moon mirror, warm lighting, and stones.

Reservations: Moderate — Book ahead where possible; otherwise aim for off-peak (early/late) and consider bar seating.

62. Seahorse

Heat 20
NYC · Eater

Seahorse is the splashy seafood brasserie from Mercer Street Hospitality and restaurateur John McDonald, which opened inside the revamped W New York in Union Square back in September 2025. 67. Odo East Village

Heat 20
NYC · Eater

70. Tera

Heat 20
NYC · Eater

This new Korean restaurant works as a daytime cafe and nighttime bar and restaurant since it opened in December. During the a.m. hours, find coffee flights, lychee co-ferment pour-overs, and tea sets.
